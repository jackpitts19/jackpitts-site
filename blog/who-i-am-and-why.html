Who I am, and why I'm doing this

I didn't grow up around deals. My dad worked in PR. My mom still works in insurance claims. One grandfather was a union electrician; the other side of the family worked for the government. Solid, respectable, entirely normal Midwestern jobs. Nobody sat me down and explained what an EBITDA multiple was over dinner, because nobody at that table had ever heard the term. As far as I know, I'm the first person in my family to actually do a deal.

So when people assume I came into this with some kind of head start, I get it, but it's not accurate. What I had was curiosity and a willingness to go find the thing nobody in my family had access to. That thing turned out to be private equity, and I went and got a seat at the table in New York.

Here's what actually changed my trajectory. I spent enough time inside PE to watch closely who was building real, durable wealth versus who was collecting a salary and a carry check while sitting somewhere near it. It wasn't the fund people. It was the owners — the operators who actually held equity in the businesses, made the calls, and captured the upside when things worked. They were also, not coincidentally, having a lot more fun than the analysts modeling their deals.

That observation is the entire reason Salt Creek Advisory and HelmIQ exist. I'd rather help owners get the outcomes they deserve and build the tools that make dealmaking better than spend a career adjacent to the wealth creation instead of inside it. Not because I think I'm owed anything — nobody handed me this — but because I saw where the real work and the real reward actually live, and I wanted to be there.

— Jack Pitts
